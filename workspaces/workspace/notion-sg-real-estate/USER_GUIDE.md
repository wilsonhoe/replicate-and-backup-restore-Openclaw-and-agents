## 1. Introduction

### What This System Is

The Door Knocking Notion System is a simple digital tool that helps you:

- **Track every door knocking session** you complete
- **Record details about people you meet** (leads)
- **Remember who to follow up with** and when
- **See how well you're performing** over time
- **Improve your results** by learning from your data

Think of it as your digital notebook that does the math for you.

### Why This System Is Useful

Without a system, most agents:
- Forget who they spoke to
- Lose track of promising leads
- Don't know which areas work best
- Can't remember what they said to each person
- Miss follow-up opportunities

With this system, you will:
- Never lose a lead's contact information
- Always know who to call back and when
- See which neighborhoods give the best results
- Track your improvement over time
- Close more deals through better organization

### What You Will Achieve

By using this system consistently, you can expect to:

- **Increase your lead conversion rate** by 20-40%
- **Reduce forgotten follow-ups** to nearly zero
- **Identify your best-performing areas** (Punggol vs. Serangoon)
- **Improve your scripts** based on actual data
- **Feel more confident** with a clear daily plan

## 2. Getting Started with Notion

### What Is Notion?

Notion is a free app that works like a digital notebook combined with a smart spreadsheet. Think of it as:

- A notebook that syncs across your phone and computer
- A filing system that finds anything instantly
- A calculator that tracks your numbers automatically
- A planner that reminds you what to do next

**Good news:** The free version has everything you need.

### How to Open the System

1. **Download the Notion app** on your phone (App Store or Google Play)
2. **Create a free account** using your email
3. **Open the link** shared with you to access the Door Knocking System
4. **Bookmark the page** so you can find it quickly

### Basic Navigation

#### Pages
- The system is organized into **pages** (like folders in a filing cabinet)
- The main page is your **Dashboard** — start here every day
- Click any page title to open it

#### Databases (The Spreadsheets)
- A database looks like a table with rows and columns
- Each row is one **record** (one person, one session, one follow-up)
- You can click any row to see more details

#### Clicking Into Items
- **Tap once** on any row to open the full details
- **Tap outside** the box to close it
- **Scroll down** to see all the information

#### Editing Fields
- **Tap any field** to edit it
- **Type your information**
- **Tap elsewhere** to save automatically
- Notion saves everything instantly — no need to press "Save"

#### Mobile Tips
- Use **voice-to-text** to enter notes quickly
- **Take photos** of unit numbers and save them in lead records
- The app works **offline** and syncs when you reconnect

## 3. System Overview

Your system has four main parts. Here's what each one does in simple terms:

### 🎯 Door Knocking Sessions

**What it is:** Your daily activity log

**Think of it as:** A workout tracker, but for door knocking

**What you record:**
- Which block you visited
- How many doors you knocked
- How many people opened the door
- How many conversations you had
- How many leads you collected

**Why it helps:** Shows you your "open rate" and which times work best

---

### 👤 Leads

**What it is:** The people you meet who might buy or sell

**Think of it as:** Your contact list, but smarter

**What you record:**
- Name and contact number
- Unit and block
- Are they owner or tenant?
- Are they looking to sell, buy, or just exploring?
- When do they want to move?
- How interested are they? (High/Medium/Low priority)

**Why it helps:** You never forget who you met or what they said

---

### 📞 Follow-Ups

**What it is:** Your reminder system for next actions

**Think of it as:** A to-do list that knows your leads

**What you record:**
- Who to contact
- When to contact them
- How to contact them (call, WhatsApp, visit)
- What happened when you contacted them
- What to do next

**Why it helps:** Ensures no lead falls through the cracks

---

### 🏠 Dashboard

**What it is:** Your control center

**Think of it as:** The cockpit of an airplane — everything important in one view

**What you see:**
- Today's follow-ups
- Hot leads (high priority)
- This week's sessions
- Quick links to scripts and resources

**Why it helps:** Start your day knowing exactly what to focus on

## 4. How to Use During Door Knocking

This section covers the complete workflow from start to finish.

### Before Your Session

**Step 1: Open the Dashboard**
- Open Notion on your phone
- Navigate to the Door Knocking System
- Look at the "Today's Execution" section

**Step 2: Create a New Session**
- Go to the "Door Knocking Sessions" database
- Tap the "+ New" button (usually at the bottom)
- Fill in:
  - **Date:** Today
  - **Area:** Punggol, Serangoon, or Sengkang
  - **Block:** The block number you're visiting (e.g., "123A")
  - **Time Slot:** Morning, Afternoon, or Evening
- Leave the numbers blank for now

**Step 3: Review Your Scripts**
- Go to the "Scripts" database
- Filter by your target area (Punggol or Serangoon)
- Read through the opening lines
- Practice your key phrases

**Step 4: Check Your Supplies**
- Flyers/market reports
- Business cards
- Phone charged
- Notion app ready

---

### During Your Session

**Quick Entry Method (Between Doors)**

You don't need to enter data while walking. Just:
1. **Take quick voice notes** on your phone
2. **Note unit numbers** on paper or phone
3. **Photo anything important**

**If You Get a Lead**

Immediately create a lead record:

1. Open the "Leads" database
2. Tap "+ New"
3. Enter quickly:
   - **Name:** What they told you (or "Block 123A Unit 12-34" if they didn't give a name)
   - **Unit:** Their unit number
   - **Block:** Current block
   - **Contact:** Phone number (or "Need to get")
   - **Intent:** Sell / Buy / Exploring
   - **Priority:** High (if very interested), Medium, or Low
   - **Linked Session:** Select the session you created earlier
4. Save and continue

**Tip:** Speed matters. Just capture the basics. You can add details after.

---

### After Your Session

**Step 1: Complete Your Session Numbers (Within 1 Hour)**

Go back to the session you created:
- **Doors Knocked:** Count from your notes
- **Doors Opened:** How many people answered
- **Conversations:** How many actual chats you had
- **Leads Captured:** How many contact details you got
- **Appointments Set:** How many agreed to meet later

The system will automatically calculate:
- **Open Rate:** % of doors that opened
- **Conversation Rate:** % of opens that turned into conversations
- **Lead Rate:** % of conversations that became leads

**Step 2: Add Details to Leads**

For each lead you captured:
- Open their record
- Add any details you remember
- Note what they said, their timeline, budget hints
- Update status if needed

**Step 3: Schedule Follow-Ups**

For each lead:
1. Go to "Follow-Ups" database
2. Create a new follow-up:
   - **Lead Name:** Select from dropdown
   - **Follow-Up Date:** When you will contact them (usually 1-3 days later)
   - **Method:** Call, WhatsApp, or Visit
   - **Priority:** Match the lead priority
   - **Status:** Pending
3. Save

**Step 4: Log Training (Optional)**

If you practiced scripts:
- Go to "Training" database
- Log your practice session
- Rate yourself 1-10
- Note what to improve

## 5. Managing Leads

### How to Add a New Lead

1. Open the **Leads** database
2. Tap the **+** button at the bottom
3. Fill in the fields:

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Name** | Their name or identifier | "Tan Wei Ming" or "Block 123A Owner" |
| **Unit** | Unit number | "12-345" |
| **Block** | Block number | "123A" |
| **Contact** | Phone number | "9123-4567" or "Need to get" |
| **Owner/Tenant** | Select one | Owner |
| **Intent** | Their goal | Sell / Buy / Exploring |
| **Timeline** | When they want to move | "3-6 months" |
| **Budget/Price** | Their price range | "$650k-$700k" |
| **Source** | How you met them | "Door knocking" |
| **Status** | Current stage | New / Follow-up / Viewing |
| **Last Contact Date** | When you last spoke | Today's date |
| **Area** | Estate location | Punggol / Serangoon |
| **Priority** | How hot is this lead | High / Medium / Low |

### How to Update Lead Status

When a lead progresses:

1. **Open their record** (tap on their name)
2. **Update the Status field:**
   - **New:** Just met them
   - **Follow-up:** You've contacted them once
   - **Viewing:** Scheduled or completed a property viewing
   - **Closed:** Deal completed
   - **Lost:** They're no longer interested
3. **Update Last Contact Date** to today
4. **Add notes** about what happened

### How to Identify Serious Prospects

Look for these signs in your Leads database:

**High Priority Indicators:**
- Timeline is specific ("within 3 months")
- They mentioned specific needs
- They asked about your services
- They gave you their contact willingly
- They're the owner (not tenant)

**Filter View: "Hot Leads"**
- Go to Leads database
- Look for the "Hot Leads" view (already created)
- This shows only High priority leads
- Focus your energy here first

## 6. Follow-Up System

### How to Schedule Follow-Ups

**Rule of thumb:** Follow up within 24-48 hours while you're still fresh in their memory.

**Creating a Follow-Up:**

1. Open **Follow-Ups** database
2. Tap **+ New**
3. Enter:
   - **Lead Name:** Select the person from the dropdown
   - **Follow-Up Date:** When you will contact them
   - **Method:** 
     - **Call** for high-priority leads
     - **WhatsApp** for quick updates
     - **Visit** if you're in the area again
   - **Priority:** Match the lead's priority
   - **Status:** Pending
4. Save

### How to Track Conversations

When you complete a follow-up:

1. Open the follow-up record
2. Change **Status** to "Done"
3. Fill in **Outcome:**
   - What they said
   - Any new information
   - Their current interest level
4. Fill in **Next Action:**
   - What you promised to do
   - When you'll contact them again
   - Any documents to send

### How to Know Who to Contact Today

**Use the "Due Today" View:**

1. Open **Follow-Ups** database
2. Switch to the "Due Today" view (at the top)
3. This shows only follow-ups scheduled for today
4. Work through this list first

**Also Check "Overdue":**

1. Switch to "Overdue" view
2. These are follow-ups you missed
3. Contact these people first (apologize for delay)

**Daily Routine:**
- Check "Due Today" every morning
- Complete or reschedule each item
- Create new follow-ups for next steps

## 7. Understanding Your Performance

### Key Numbers Explained

Your system tracks five important numbers. Here's what they mean:

#### 1. Doors Knocked
**What it is:** Total doors you knocked on

**Why it matters:** Shows your activity level

**Good target:** 40-50 doors per session

---

#### 2. Doors Opened
**What it is:** How many people answered the door

**Why it matters:** Shows if you're knocking at good times

**Good target:** 35%+ open rate (at least 15 out of 50)

---

#### 3. Conversations
**What it is:** How many actual chats you had

**Why it matters:** Shows your opening line is working

**Good target:** 50%+ conversation rate (at least 8 out of 15 opens)

---

#### 4. Leads Captured
**What it is:** How many contact details you collected

**Why it matters:** This is your future business

**Good target:** 25%+ lead rate (at least 2 out of 8 conversations)

---

#### 5. Appointments Set
**What it is:** How many agreed to meet again

**Why it matters:** These are your hottest prospects

**Good target:** 40%+ of leads should become appointments

---

### What Good Looks Like (Benchmarks)

**Weekly Targets:**

| Metric | Target | If Below, Try... |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| Sessions per week | 4-5 | Schedule specific days |
| Doors per session | 40-50 | Stay longer or move faster |
| Open Rate | >35% | Try different times (evenings work best) |
| Conversation Rate | >50% | Practice your opening line |
| Lead Rate | >25% | Ask better qualifying questions |
| Appointment Rate | >40% | Be more specific about next steps |

**Monthly Targets:**

- **100+ doors knocked** (2-3 sessions per week)
- **20+ leads generated**
- **5+ appointments set**
- **1-2 deals closed**

---

### Reading Your Weekly Reviews

The system automatically calculates your rates. Here's how to interpret them:

**Example:**

```
Doors Knocked: 50
Doors Opened: 20 (40% open rate ✓)
Conversations: 12 (60% conversation rate ✓)
Leads: 4 (33% lead rate ✓)
```

**All green?** Keep doing what you're doing!

**Open rate low?** Try different times or blocks

**Conversation rate low?** Practice your opening script

**Lead rate low?** Ask more qualifying questions

## 9. Weekly Review

### Why Weekly Reviews Matter

After one week of data, you can start seeing patterns:

- Which areas give better results?
- What times work best?
- Which scripts are most effective?
- How are you improving?

### How to Review Your Week

**Step 1: Look at Your Numbers**

Go to "Weekly Reviews" database:
- Check your totals for the week
- Compare to previous weeks
- Look at your rates (Open, Conversation, Lead)

**Step 2: Identify Patterns**

Ask yourself:
- Which area had the best lead rate?
- Which time slot had the most opens?
- Which day of the week worked best?

**Step 3: Adjust Your Approach**

Based on your data:
- Focus more on high-performing areas
- Adjust your timing
- Practice scripts that need work
- Set goals for next week

### Comparing Areas: Punggol vs. Serangoon

**Punggol (Young Families):**
- Best time: 6:30-8pm (after work)
- Tone: Energetic, opportunity-focused
- Hook: MOP completion, upgrading
- Response: Usually higher energy, more curious

**Serangoon (Mature Owners):**
- Best time: 7-8:30pm
- Tone: Calm, advisory, authoritative
- Hook: Infrastructure, value preservation
- Response: More cautious, need trust first

**What to Track:**
- Compare open rates between areas
- Which gives more high-priority leads?
- Where do you feel more comfortable?

### Script Effectiveness

In the "Scripts" database, track:
- **Usage Count:** How many times you used it
- **Effectiveness Score:** Rate 1-10 after each session
- **Notes:** What worked, what to change

**Monthly Review:**
- Which script has the highest average score?
- Which gets the most positive responses?
- Which needs rewriting?

## 10. Tips for Success

### Keep Entries Simple

**Don't overthink it.** Quick notes are better than perfect notes.

**Instead of:**
> "Met Mr. Tan at 7:15pm. He was wearing a blue shirt. Very nice man. Said he's lived here 5 years. Wife works at the hospital. Two kids in primary school. Thinking about upgrading to condo because they need more space. Mentioned budget around $650k. Seemed genuinely interested but wants to think about it."

**Just write:**
> "Mr. Tan, owner 5 years. Wants to upgrade, budget ~$650k. Genuinely interested, needs time to think."

**The goal:** Capture enough to remember the conversation, not write a novel.

---

### Update Daily

**Consistency beats perfection.**

- 5 minutes every day > 1 hour once a week
- Daily updates keep the information fresh
- Builds the habit faster
- You'll actually use the data

**Set a reminder:** Phone alarm for "Update Notion" after each session.

---

### Focus on Consistency, Not Perfection

**Week 1-2:** Just log sessions and leads

**Week 3-4:** Add follow-ups and review numbers

**Month 2+:** Analyze patterns, refine scripts

**Don't try to do everything perfectly from day one.** Start simple and build up.

---
