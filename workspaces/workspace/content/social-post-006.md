## LinkedIn Version

I see this mistake constantly.

Someone spends 10 hours building an automation for a task that takes 5 minutes weekly.

That's not efficiency. That's ego.

**Before automating anything, ask:**

1. **Is it repetitive?**
   - One-time tasks = manual
   - Repeated tasks = automation candidate

2. **Does it follow rules?**
   - Predictable logic = automate
   - Requires judgment = manual

3. **Is volume high enough?**
   - 5 min/week = don't automate (costs more to build)
   - 5 min/day = consider automation
   - 1 hr/day = automate immediately

**The Automation ROI Threshold:**

If (Time to Build) > (Time Saved in 3 Months), don't automate.

Save your automation energy for high-volume, rule-based, repetitive tasks (Digital Applied, 2026).
